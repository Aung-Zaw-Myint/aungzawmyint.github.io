Dr. Aung Zaw Myint

Researcher

Specializing in the mathematical analysis of reaction-diffusion models, free boundary problems, and complex biological networks. Scholarly matrix spanning research deployments across Myanmar, China, Germany, and Thailand.

ORCID ResearchGate Clarivate (Web of Science) Elsevier (Scopus / ScienceDirect)

Dr. Aung Zaw Myint serves as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Sirindhorn International Institute of Technology (SIIT), Thammasat University, Thailand. Concurrently, he maintains his appointment as Associate Professor of Mathematics at the University of Mandalay, Myanmar, where he has been a faculty member since 2021. He completed his Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at the Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT), China, specializing in Mathematical Biology. His doctoral research, supervised by Prof. Wang Mingxin and co-supervised by Li Li, culminated in his dissertation titled “Qualitative Analysis of Some Reaction–Diffusion Systems,” focusing on structural analysis and analytical parameters of nonlinear diffusion mechanics.

His core investigations explore structural traits of partial differential equations (PDEs), free boundary mechanics, dynamic population tracking, spatial heterogeneity, and epidemic networks. His published tracks deal heavily with global existence frameworks, multi-species boundary mappings, a priori estimates, and topological bifurcations branching out from variable semi-trivial equilibria models.
