Profile

Dr. Aung Zaw Myint is currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at Sirindhorn International Institute of Technology, Thammasat University, Thailand. He has also served as an Associate Professor of Mathematics at the University of Mandalay, Myanmar, since 2021. He received his Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from Harbin Institute of Technology, China, specializing in Mathematical Biology with a focus on partial differential equations (PDE) models. His doctoral dissertation, “Qualitative Analysis of Some Reaction–Diffusion Systems,” investigated the qualitative behavior and mathematical structures of nonlinear diffusion processes arising in biological systems. His supervisor is Prof. Wang Mingxin, and his co-supervisor is Li Li. His research interests include mathematical biology, reaction–diffusion systems, free boundary problems, nonlinear partial differential equations, and dynamical systems. He has academic and research experience across Myanmar, China, Germany, and Thailand. Dr. Aung Zaw Myint is a researcher in Applied Mathematics focusing on nonlinear partial differential equations, dynamical systems, free boundary problems, reaction-diffusion systems, and mathematical biology. Currently, Research interests encompass predator–prey models, reaction–diffusion systems, chemotaxis models, and free boundary problems. His work emphasizes the global existence, uniqueness, and a priori estimates of nonnegative solutions, as well as the existence, bifurcation, and stability of non-constant steady-state solutions emerging from constant semi-trivial equilibria. He further investigates the spreading and vanishing dynamics in free-boundary problems associated with population, disease, and ecological models.
